The International Workshop on Gene Editing in Zebrafish

From Superbugs to Cardiovascular Diseases: One Health at Genomic Resolution

September 30 - October 3, Sousse, Tunisia

REGISTER

The International Workshop on Gene Editing in Zebrafish, organized by Learn to Win and Multiverse AI in partnership with ISA Chott Mariem, marks a significant milestone in genomic advancement. Facilitated by seasoned professionals, this workshop aims to introduce the zebrafish model to students, academics, researchers, and healthcare professionals in Tunisia. Additionally, it will utilize CRISPR technology and the zebrafish model to study human and animal diseases, focusing on the one health approach.

Drawing from the achievements of previous CRIPR’23 events, CRIPR’24 is poised to further explore the intersection of biotechnology and deep learning in gene editing, featuring hands-on training sessions for practical application.

September 30 - October 3 2024, Monday-Friday

CRISPER Applications

Clustered regularly interspaced short palindromic repeats (CRISPR) system provides adaptive immunity against plasmids and phages in prokaryotes. This system inspires the development of a powerful genome engineering tool, the CRISPR/CRISPR-associated nuclease 9 (CRISPR/Cas9) genome editing system.
