Introduction:

In recent years, zebrafish have emerged as a new animal model for research. Its advantages include ease of use, low maintenance costs and high throughput. As a vertebrate model, the zebrafish resembles human physiology in many ways. Zebrafish is widely used in developmental biology, cancer, toxicology, genetic disorders, neuronal disorders, diabetes and cardiovascular research. With the introduction of CRISPR technology and its ongoing demystification in African labs, there's a growing interest from research institutions in Tunisia in particular and Africa in general to use this model.

The aim of this workshop program is, firstly, to introduce this relatively new animal model to students, academics, researchers and health professionals in Tunisia; secondly, to establish a platform for working with the model and how to take advantage of it and build national and international collaboration; thirdly, to use CRISPR technology and the zebrafish model to study both cardiovascular disease and antibiotic resistance.

This workshop is jointly organized by Learn & Win and the Higher Institute of Agronomy Chott-Meriem, Sousse University. The event will include both theoretical lectures from renowned experts in the field, as well as a hands-on session to train interested researchers.

Why you should attend:

  • The biggest zebrafish workshop program in the region.
  • A great opportunity to learn about the advantages of using this new animal model for research.
  • To explore how zebrafish research contributes to human therapy for heart disease and antibiotics resistance.
  • A chance to network with leading zebrafish professionals, scientists and researchers and to get updated on cutting-edge research in a diverse array of topics.
  • Provides the chance for new and junior researchers and scientists, or students to be part of hands-on practical sessions.
  • To provide a platform for collaborations and to broaden the scope of science related to the zebrafish animal model.

What you will learn:

The event will involve both theoretical lectures from renowned experts in the field as well as hands-on practical sessions to train interested researchers. The learning outcomes are:

  • Introduction to the zebrafish as an animal model and its use as a model in different areas of research compared to other models.
  • Learn about the emerging and most recent research work done on the use of zebrafish, including human disease models.
  • Establishment of a zebrafish platform including zebrafish system maintenance and water quality monitoring, fluorescence and confocal imaging of zebrafish, mRNA and morpholino antisense oligonucleotide injections for protein knockdown or overexpression, crispr-cas9 genome editing technology and toxicology assays.
